In the aerospace industry, one of the key processes in aircraft manufacturing is drilling components. Currently, this process is carried out in two ways: manually by an operator or with a specific machine. However, these traditional machines lack flexibility, as they are designed for specific tasks and cannot adapt to different aircraft parts.

My work was part of a larger project aimed at solving this lack of flexibility in aerospace manufacturing automation. This was a technical challenge that required the use of advanced computer vision and robotic control techniques.
